How did I get here?

"Life begins at 40" At first, I thought this is just a passing quote by those who missed out on their younger years. But now that I am walking on this path, I found it insightful and now think 40 as a moment of rebirth, a chance to embark on new adventures and time to embrace one's forgotten passion and potential.

Career Journey

High School Teacher

Never in my wildest dream that my first job after college is becoming a high school Teacher.

I am only 19 at the time and the one I taught on my first year are 16 year olds! I still remember the very first time I enter the classroom and they taught I am a visitor üòÅ.

Manufaturing Roles

While it's both fun and rewarding being a Teacher but after 2 years, I have decided to move on and enter the world of Manufacturing. I started as a machine operator and later used my CAD skills to become a CADMAC technician. Sounds technical but all I do is draw the parts in a computer and program so that it is machined properly.

Later, I was involed in Production planning. But the job seems to be repetetive so I decided to go back to the tools and become a hydraulic technician in Moog Controls. This is my dream job. Since I love problem solving, I really enjoyed the task of troubleshooting a faulty equipment and applying my skills and knowledge to make it work again. That feeling of solving the task and the accomplishment I felt is so satisfying.

However, as for all things... I started looking for other adventures, and that is where I stumbled upon, you guess it, Web Development!

The Future

I immediately realized the vast potential being a Software Developer.

Using software, I can do the following:

  • Make an impact globally by creating an app that solves a real-world problem.
  • Help Businesses improve their efficiency by automating repetitive tasks.
  • Increase the presence of organizations I believe in by making a great website and make it known globally.

And so, my Web development journey commenced...

Self Study

During the Pandemic, I started to fall in love on the idea to become a Web developer and dipped my foot on endless YouTube tutorials. It was confusing at first, but everytime I get something working, that feeling of creating a program to do something for you excites me and made me more convinced that this is my next career shift and probably the last one.

Job Hunting and the Reality

After thinking, I know enough, I started applying for any software / IT jobs I can find on Seek and LinkedIn and to my diappointment, I got zero job offer. Sure enough, the competition is fierce and my skills are still subpar compared to other entry level applicants.

And in my quest to improve, I started looking for scholarships here in Victoria that can help me bridge that gap both in Technical and Personal Level, ... and that's how I found Holberton School.
